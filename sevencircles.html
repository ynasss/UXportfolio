SevenCircles - contemporary art website redesign

This project entailed a comprehensive redesign of a contemporary art event website, notable for its dense textual content (8000+ words) and rich imagery.

The primary focus was centered on reorganizing the site's information architecture and presenting its content through the application of UX writing and content strategy principles, aiming to enhance accessibility and user engagement.

This led to defining a clear content hierarchy and developing focused text guidelines.

Research

Challenge

The primary challenge is to design a website that is rich in both text and imagery, yet remains effortlessly navigable and accessible.
The objective is to develop a user-friendly interface that not only allows for quick overviews but also supports in-depth exploration. This design must cater to a wide range of user requirements, from the casual browsing of an art enthusiast to the detailed analysis sought by art industry stakeholders.

Research Goals

Two surveys were conducted aiming at:

  • Identify the art-event Key Information that audiences need to evaluate their interest in an art event
  • Uncover audiences Pain Points encountered in relation to contemporary art promotional materials
  • Gain insights into general attitudes and emotional responses towards reading contemporary art event descriptions.

Research Insights

The surveys revealed specific audience attitudes and preferences:

  1. Users need immediate access to essential event information- date, location, ticketing link, event duration

  2. Preference for short yet impactful event descriptions, with concrete, relatable tone of voice

  3. People want to know what makes each event special - content, setting, or concept

  4. Event imagery evokes immediate emotional connection between the user and the event and should be prominently displayed.

Information Architecture & Layout

IA Process

To define the app's Information Architecture and identify its key features, I initially created User Flows. These subsequently evolved into a Sitemap, which was then refined and validated through an iterative process of card sorting.

Browsing

The landing page serves as the initial point for users to browse and search through tutorials and hobby categories.

Lesson Area

The contents of the Lesson Area are organized with a tab menu. The tabs contain information on the lesson's details and the video tutorials. The user could see the lesson's difficulty, duration, description, realted videos, and a contact card for support by a teacher.

Teacher's Profile

For the user's convenience, the third tab was transformed into a separate screen Teacher's Profile. This section provides information on the teacher's credentials, creative path, personal work and interests, and a way to contact them.

Testing & Iterating

Usability Testing

The app's Information Architecture and Navigation patterns underwent validation through 6 moderated Usability Tests and an A/B Test. These tests uncovered usability issues within the app's UI. Testers frequently mistook the image-carousel in the Lesson Area's, when looking for a video-tutorial. This problem could lead to confusion, frustration, and potential app abandonment. This issue also hinders the discoverability of the tab menu.

Solution

The solution implemented a more visually prominent tab menu and a distinctive image carousel. Borrowed from the principles of the well tested Material Design 3 design system the itterated UI improved the video discoverability, and enhanced the findability of the tab pattern.

High-fidelity Interactive Prototype

Accessibility

To provide an immersive experience for all individuals interested in the QualyTime project, I developed a high-fidelity Interactive Prototype.
To maximize the effectiveness and functionality of the product, I adhered to the Level A usability and Level AAA color contrast standards as outlined in the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines.

Personal Conclusion

Prioritizing the understanding and implementation of feedback from my mentors and colleagues proved to be a crucial decision that maintained my focus on achieving the final result. This approach not only deepened my comprehension of human-centered design-thinking but also enabled me to successfully apply it to my ideas.
