SevenCircles - contemporary art website redesign

This project entailed a comprehensive redesign of a contemporary art event website, notable for its dense textual content (8000+ words) and rich imagery.

The primary focus was centered on reorganizing the site's information architecture and presenting its content through the application of UX writing and content strategy principles, aiming to enhance accessibility and user engagement.

This led to defining a clear content hierarchy and developing focused text guidelines.

Research

Challenge

The primary challenge is to design a website that is rich in both text and imagery, yet remains effortlessly navigable and accessible.
The objective is to develop a user-friendly interface that not only allows for quick overviews but also supports in-depth exploration. This design must cater to a wide range of user requirements, from the casual browsing of an art enthusiast to the detailed analysis sought by art industry stakeholders.

Research Goals

Two surveys were conducted aiming at:

  • Identify the art-event Key Information that audiences need to evaluate their interest in an art event
  • Uncover audiences Pain Points encountered in relation to contemporary art promotional materials
  • Gain insights into general attitudes and emotional responses towards reading contemporary art event descriptions.

Research Insights

The surveys revealed specific audience attitudes and preferences:

  1. Users need immediate access to essential event information- date, location, ticketing link, event duration

  2. Preference for short yet impactful event descriptions, with concrete, relatable tone of voice

  3. People want to know what makes each event special - content, setting, or concept

  4. Event imagery evokes immediate emotional connection between the user and the event and should be prominently displayed.

Information Architecture & Layout

Structure & Navigation

The websites navigation was designed with a fixed menu layout, providing easy overview of all page categories. Art industry stakeholders often want to gain a detailed understanding of collaborations, projects, and their artists so I further interlinked those pages, making all available information a couple clicks away.

General Layout

The two independently scrollable panes are an important feature that:

  • allows users to independently explore text and images, enhancing user autonomy and reducing cognitive load
  • simplifies navigation through the lengthy content, dividing it in two shortens the pages length
  • maximally utilizes the screen real estate, enhancing the page content organization

Content Strategy & layout

The layout allows for scannable display in accordance with the content hierarchy defined by the research. The following example is an event's page featuring a title, a subtitle, 8 images, a brief event description, a detailed artistic concept, and links to 9 featured artists

Designing & Building

UI Aesthetics

The minimalistic design philosophy aimed at creating a calm, clean, and uncluttered space, where the art's imagery takes central stage while in harmony with the text contents.

Development

Once the interactive prototype was finished in Figma, the initial static version of the website was built locally using HTML, CSS, and JS. Later the the static site was transformed into a dynamic one, using the WordPress environment locally, to be migrated later to the official domain for final adjustments.
