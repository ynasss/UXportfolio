QualyTime - educational app for hobbyists

An app that empowers people to engage in recreational activities in a productive, time-efficient, and inspiring way.

It provides hobbyists of all levels with carefully curated information and guidance to learn, advance, and make the most of their precious free time.

It offers an array of inspiring instructional videos as well as easy access to expert advice.

Research & Discovery

Background

The objective of my Careerfoundry’s course was to build an “Expert” app that connects the user to an expert in any given field.
I decided to design the app from the perspective of a hobbyist.

Research Goals

To understand how people approach new or old hobbies, and find out what might help them, I conducted user interviews, focusing on:

  • How do people choose, prepare, start, and progress in an activity;
  • What supports people best in their hobbies- materials, best practices etc;
  • When do people feel trust towards information sources in unfamiliar fields;

Research informed hypothesis

Informed by the research insights I've concluded that
Hobbyists would need avideo-based learning platform, whose content creators serve as teachers and advisors, thereby fostering skill advancement and time optimization.

Minimal viable product

Based on the conducted user research, an informed hypothesis, 2 user personas, and 2 user journey maps, I concluded that the most useful MVP for hobbyists would provide:

  • A way to browse, sort and filter curated video lessons;
  • A Lesson Area containing information on the lesson, its teacher, video tutorials, and support materials;
  • A way to book a call and/or start a chat with a teacher;
  • A teachers profile with personal infos.

Ideation

IA Process

To define the app's Information Architecture and identify its key features, I initially created User Flows. These subsequently evolved into a Sitemap, which was then refined and validated through an iterative process of card sorting.

Browsing

The landing page serves as the initial point for users to browse and search through tutorials and hobby categories.

Lesson Area

The contents of the Lesson Area are organized with a tab menu. The tabs contain information on the lesson's details and the video tutorials. The user could see the lesson's difficulty, duration, description, realted videos, and a contact card for support by a teacher.

Teacher's Profile

For the user's convenience, the third tab was transformed into a separate screen Teacher's Profile. This section provides information on the teacher's credentials, creative path, personal work and interests, and a way to contact them.

Testing & Iterating

Usability Testing

The app's Information Architecture and Navigation patterns underwent validation through 6 moderated Usability Tests and an A/B Test. These tests uncovered usability issues within the app's UI. Testers frequently mistook the image-carousel in the Lesson Area's, when looking for a video-tutorial. This problem could lead to confusion, frustration, and potential app abandonment. This issue also hinders the discoverability of the tab menu.

Solution

The solution implemented a more visually prominent tab menu and a distinctive image carousel. Borrowed from the principles of the well tested Material Design 3 design system the solution improved the video discoverability, and enhanced the findability of the tab pattern.

High-fidelity Prototype

To provide an immersive experience for all individuals interested in the QualyTime project, I developed a high-fidelity prototype. To maximize the effectiveness and functionality of the product, I adhered to the Level A usability and Level AAA color contrast standards as outlined in the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines.
